# Review rubric

Score each dimension from 1 to 5:

- Thesis: clear, contestable, appropriately scoped.
- Evidence: authoritative, sufficient, accurately cited.
- Reasoning: warrants explicit; alternatives and causality handled.
- Counterargument: strongest relevant objection treated fairly.
- Structure: each section advances the argument.
- Insight: synthesis changes how the question can be understood.
- Prose: precise, readable, free of inflated certainty.

Decision thresholds:

- `pass`: no material factual or logical defect; remaining edits are optional.
- `minor_revision`: thesis stands, but localized evidence, clarity, or structure changes are required.
- `major_revision`: central thesis, evidence base, reasoning chain, or treatment of objections must materially change.

Priority order: factual accuracy, reasoning validity, source integrity, thesis/structure, prose polish.
