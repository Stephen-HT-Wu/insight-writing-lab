# Source policy

Rank evidence by authority and proximity to the claim:

1. Primary law, official statistics, standards, court opinions, original datasets, and first-party records.
2. Peer-reviewed research, academic presses, and established research institutions.
3. Official technical or organizational documentation.
4. Newsrooms with named authors, corrections, and editorial accountability.
5. Identified expert analysis with transparent evidence.

Use encyclopedias, aggregators, blogs, and social posts to discover leads, not as the sole support for a consequential claim. Prefer two independent sources for important claims unless one authoritative primary source is sufficient. Consider publication date, conflicts of interest, methodology, sample, jurisdiction, and whether the source actually supports the sentence being written.
